Indrayani

(30kg loose)

Indrayani rice is a type of aromatic rice that is grown in Maharashtra, India. It has a medium-sized grain and a sticky texture when cooked. It is named after the Indrayani river that flows through the region where it is cultivated Indrayani rice is used for making various dishes such as curries, pulao, and sweet dishes. It is also popular for making a thick soup of rice and milk called ‘Bhatachi Pej’ which is given to children, elderly people and patients

Indrayani Kani

(30kg Loose)

Indrayani kani is a type of rice that is grown in Maharashtra, India. It is a variety of Indrayani rice, which is an aromatic and sticky rice named after the Indrayani river. Indrayani kani is a medium-sized grain that is used for making various dishes such as khichadi, pulao, and sweet dishes Indrayani kani is a hybrid of Ambemohar rice, which is another fragrant rice variety from Maharashtra that has a strong aroma of mango blossoms. Indrayani kani was developed by Rice Research Centre near Lonavala in 1987 to improve the yield and disease resistance of Ambemohar rice

Steam Kolam

Steam kolam is a medium-sized grain that is steamed before milling, which gives it a fluffy texture and a mild fragrance Steam kolam rice is used for making various dishes such as khichadi, pulao, and sweet dishes. It is also popular for making a thick soup of rice and milk called ‘Bhatachi Pej’ which is given to children, elderly people and patients

Raw Wada Kolam

Raw wada kolam is a type of rice that is grown in Maharashtra, India. It is an aromatic and sticky rice that has a small grain size and a mild fragrance. It is named after the Wada region in Palghar district, where it is mainly cultivated. Raw wada kolam rice is a hybrid of Ambemohar rice, which is another fragrant rice variety from Maharashtra that has a strong aroma of mango blossoms.

Basmati 1121

Basmati 1121 is an independently derived Basmati rice variety, evolved through the process of hybridization over a long breeding process. It relatively requires less water and matures early than traditional Basmati cultivars. When cooked, the rice does not turn sticky, and possesses minimum breadth-wise expansion. Cooked rice is fluffy white with pleasant aroma and very tasty to eat.
