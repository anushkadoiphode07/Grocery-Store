Wheat Rawa

ravva, is a wheat product and a form of semolina. Rava is made by grinding husked wheat and is used in Indian cuisine to make rava dosa, rava idli, upma, khichdis, and also sweets like rava ladoo and rava halwa also known as ravakesari.

Corn Poha

Makai poha is part of Indian Gujarati cuisine. It can also be referred to as corn or maize flakes, but unlike the breakfast cereal cornflakes, these are not ready to eat. Makka poha is usually fried in hot oil so it puffs up, for consumption as a snack. Poha is a wholesome meal. It is a good source of carbohydrates and iron, rich in fibre, a good source of antioxidants and essential vitamins and is gluten-free.

White Poha/Beaten Rice

Beaten rice or Poha is nothing but flattened rice. Rice is first parboiled and then rolled and flattened out to get poha. Poha can be of varying thickness depending on the amount of pressure that is applied while and this thick or thin poha is then used to make various dishes as a main ingredient.

Peanut Chikki

Chikki is a traditional Indian sweet (brittle) generally made from peanuts and jaggery. There are several different varieties of chikki in addition to the most common groundnut (peanut) chikki.

Wheat Daliya

Daliya is a tasty and nutritious dessert made of broken wheat (daliya) cooked in milk. Broken or cracked wheat is an excellent source of fiber. Daliya is a very nutritious and healthy dish with all the benefits of whole-grains.
